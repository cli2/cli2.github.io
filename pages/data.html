Roles

Individual Work

Given Names are different between China and the US

In China and most other East Asian countries, first names are not chosen from a pool. We do not usually have name after others and want to have unique first names with good meanings. However, in most western countries, there is a pool of first names (John, Mary, etc.).

Americans seem to be very fond of movies. So, is there any correlations between famous movies stars and baby names given in a certain year?

Name Datasets

For the actors/actresses' names, I intend to use the IMDB 5000 movies dataset which contains three leading actors/actresses' names of each movie and the publishing year. In addition, I plan to add academy award best leading actors' and best leading actresses' names to the corresponding year by parsing the Wikipedia page. For the baby names, I found the dataset in CSV at https://www.kaggle.com/kaggle/us-baby-names. This dataset contains the ranking of baby names from 1880.

Data Manipulation

I parsed the IMDB dataset and only stored the stars in the top 5 movies in every year and joined it with the Academy Award dataset in the format of:
{‘year’:[imdb1, imdb2, imdb3, imdb4, imdb5, bestActor, bestActress]}

I manipulated the Baby name dataset and stored the data in a dictionary with the format of:
{‘name’:[(‘year’,’number’),(‘year’,’number’),(‘year’,’number’),(‘year’,’number’),(‘year’,’number’)]}

Visualization Phase 1

I chose the top occurrence name 'Robert' to conduct pilot analysis and visualization to see if the name did have strong impact on US baby names. The different-colored dots are the year a star named 'Robert' appeared.

As a result, it seemed that 'Robert' has significant impact on both girl and boy names. Therefore, I decided to further analyze the correlation between movie star names and baby names.

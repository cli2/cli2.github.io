About NSportC

NSportC is a combination of web and mobile app that helps middle school and high school coaches handle potential concussions in the right way. Furthermore, NSportC will also help teenager sports players and their parents keep pre-medical information.

Timeline and Methods

The Problem - 'In Suspicion' of Concussion

In high school sports teams, players can easily fall down and might get concussion. However, how to tell if a player is still healthy to play or is in suspicion of concussion is very hard for high school coaches since there is no professional medical teams in high school. In addition, it is hard to manage player's physical conditions and previous concussion records. Therefore, NSportC is very helpful in handling these conditions.

However, in our research, we find two major legal regulations:

  • If a teenager player is in suspicion of having concussion, the coach should remove the player from play immediately.
  • Only medical professionals should determine whether a teenager player has concussion.

User Interviews

We kicked off the user research right after the research. To confirm our assumption, we interviewed with two coaches, Joe and Max. Both of them have been high school coaches for over 10 years, and they have children that play in high school teams. From our user interviews, we confirmed our assumption and realized that our product could potentially help both coaches and players' parents.

Major Findings:

  • Coaches can hardly tell whether a player should be "suspected" to have concussion.
  • Although state laws regulates that coaches must go through a concussion training, but only a few can remember the details of the training.
  • Parents think the pre-medical records are important even if the incident does not lead to concussion, because they believe that the real problem is the overall brain health, not how many concussions happened in the past.

Primary Personas

After interviewing with coaches, we decided to design two persona types - primary persona for middle school or high school coaches and secondary persona for teenager players' parents.

For our primary persona type, we developed two personas varying by their coaching experience. This variable could greatly affect the behavior and experience using our product. A more experienced coach may have more awareness of each player's health status, while a less experienced coach may miss important indicators that a player's health status is unstable.

Secondary Persona

We also developed a secondary persona to represent teenager players' parents. We discussed the personas with our client to understand more of the big picture and started drawing workflow diagrams.

Different Workflow for Mobile and Web

Using our primary persona - coach's role - as a breakthrough point, we created workflow diagrams to visualize the users end-to-end experience and map the product workflow. We decided to put the more sophisticated team management tools on the web platform, while the mobile app covers the assessment tool, since coaches only have smartphones with them during games or practices.

Assessment Tool for Concussion

We researched on concussion training materials and asked opinions from experienced coaches. To determine the mental state of a teenager player and the possibility of concussion, coaches normal ask three types of questions to the players when an incident happens:

  • Basic questions related to the player's demographical information. For example, "what is your mother's name?" and "what grade are you in?"
  • Cognitive questions to test the logical thinking of the player. For example, "can you repeat reversely of the numbers listed here?" and "can you tell me what is 3 times 5 minus 6?"
  • Motional tasks to see if the player can control the body balance. For example, "on a firm surface, stand on single foot" and "one foot behind other".

Coaches Suggested the Total Assessment Time Should Be less than 5 Minutes.

Structuring the Experience

Objectives

We listed a set of experience principles to define the "personality" of our product. The principles used here were addressed to specific user needs including functionality and usability.

Web Wireframe

The web app is mainly designed with emphasis on information management. Coaches will be able to view all players' status and previous assessment test results. In addition, coaches can print out reports based on his players' status.

App Wireframe

The mobile app covers most of the functions on the web app, but it has the assessment tool exclusively. To simplify the process and save time, the coach will be able to quickly access the assessment tool on the main page and then select the player associated with the test. The coach will also be able to quickly switch teams via the sidebar and view players' status and information.

Stremlined Information Flow

The dashboard page serves as a central information hub. Coaches can manage (view, edit, and delete) all the teams.

The team page shows the list of players in this team. Coaches can change team names and add/delete players on this page.

Organized Player Information

A player's information can be divided into two parts - demographical information and test results. The coach will be able to edit a player's demographical information as well as view or export all previous test results. On the mobile application, the coach can conduct assessment tests for the player.

Standardized Assessment

After finishing all assessment questions with 3 minutes (less than 5 minutes), the mobile app will determine whether the test is passed or failed. If the test is failed, our product will advice the coach to immediately send the player to medical professionals for further diagnosis. There will also be a shortcut button to send an email to let the player's parent know the situation.

Design Challenge -
Handling Sports Concussions in High-school

There is over 380,000 sports-related concussions in the US. Unlike professional sports teams, middle school or high school teams don’t often have medical professional on the team, so coaches are the front line to make the right judgement if an incident happens. Also, even if the incident doesn’t lead to a concussion, all pre-medical information should be recorded because they are very important to doctors.

'In suspicion' of Concussion

Timeline

As a group of 6 designers, we started our 3-month research-based design process to build a product to help high-school team coaches make right decisions and keep all pre-medical data when a player gets hit.

I worked as the lead designer for web interface. I also participated in the design of mobile app, the user research, and usability tests.

Timeline and Methods

The Research

In order to know more about our users, sports concussion scenarios, and if there is any related regulations, we kicked off our research and discovered valuable insights.

User Research

- User Interviews

We conducted interviews to understand what coaches really need. We talked with two coaches, Joe and Max, who have been high school coaches for over 10 years, Interestingly, they both also have children who played in high school teams, so they also shared their experience as parents. Here is the major findings:

  • Coaches can hardly tell if a player is in suspicious of having a concussion after being hit.
  • Although state laws regulates that coaches go through a concussion training, only a few can remember the details of the training.
  • Parents think the pre-medical records are important even if the incident does not lead to concussion.


- Personas

We built two primary personas for coaches and one secondary persona for parents based on our interviews. Coaches vary by their coaching experience. A more experienced coach have more awareness of each player's health status, while a less experienced coach may miss some important health indicators.

Inexperienced coach persona

Experienced coach persona

Parents care about children's health as well as how they recover from the concussion. In addition, they play an important role in helping recording children's recovery.

Parent persona

- Insights

  • We decided to bring parents in to the loop of the product
  • Coaches need help to determine if a player has concussion or not
  • A coach at high-school may coach more than one team

Regulations Research

Since concussion is crucial to brain health, which is a serious issue, we started to look into some federal regulations that might influence our product. In our research, we find two major legal regulations:

  • A player who is believed to have concussion should be removed from the play immediately.
  • Only medical professionals should determine whether a teenager player has concussion.
  • A player can only return to play with permission from a health care professional.

Federal regulations

- Insights

  • Parents play an important role to update students' medical records
  • Coaches need to know when a player can come back to court and manage the different teams efficiently

Assessment Tools Research

Now that we know coaches need assistance in assessing if a play is in concussion or not, we looked into various assessment tools and their pros &s; cons:

  • Sideline Concussion Assessment Tool, version 3 (SCAT3): accurate but requires professional medical help
  • King-Devick Test: requires eye-movement detector
  • Balance Error Scoring System (BESS): requires a long test test
  • Wearables: expensive to purchase
  • 5-min assessment: quick and easy to implement, with 90% accuracy

- Insights

After comparing the different tools and talking with coaches, we decided to adopt the 5-min assessment tool and build it into our product.

5-min assessment for players 'in suspicion' of concussions

The Vision

Based on our research and product explorations, we defined our product as a duo-platform application to help school coaches make right decisions and work with parents to keep all pre-medical data when a player gets hit. There are three main functionalities: Team Management; Concussion Assessment; Player Medical Records.

Product Objectives

Duo-platform Experience

We found that most coaches have a smartphone and carry it with them on court. They also often have computers in their office. Therefore, We decided to put the more sophisticated team management tools on the web platform, while the mobile app covers the assessment tool.

We created workflow diagrams to visualize the end-to-end user experience and map the product workflow. On the web, coaches and parents will follow a simple registration process and land on a customized dashboard.

Web Workflow

On the mobile app, coaches have quick access to assessment as well as an overview of player information and teams information.

Putting Together the Workflow

Web - Team Management & Records

The web app is mainly designed with emphasis on information management. Coaches will be able to view all players' status and previous assessment test results. In addition, coaches can print out reports based on his players' status.

Web wireframes and workflow for team management & records management


App - Concussion Assessment & Quick Access

The mobile has an emphasis on the assessment tool exclusively. To simplify the process and save time, the coach will be able to quickly access the assessment tool on the main page and then select the player associated with the test.

Streamlined Team Management

The dashboard page serves as a central information hub. Coaches can manage (view, edit, and delete) all the teams.

The team page shows the list of players in this team. Coaches can change team names and add/delete players.

Organized Player Information & Records

A player's information can be divided into two parts - demographical information and test results. The coach will be able to edit a player's demographical information as well as view or export all previous test results.

Standardized Assessment

After finishing all assessment questions with 3 minutes (less than 5 minutes), the mobile app will determine whether the test is passed or failed. If the test is failed, our product will advice the coach to immediately send the player to medical professionals for further diagnosis.

Testing with Users

After finishing the prototype, we did two usability tests and fixed some usability problems. For example, on our first version, we use the alphabetical order for the player list. But we later changed it to order by status to better group the information.
